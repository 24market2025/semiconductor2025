Wirewound Potentiometers Market Overview
The Wirewound Potentiometers use a resistance element, and the copper-nickel or nickel-chromium resistance wire is evenly wound on the cable core or the card core. The combination of resistive element and precious metal alloy vernier maintains precision and stable performance. Due to their precision, these potentiometers are often used in stereo systems as well as in high power applications such as transducers and televisions. Wirewound Potentiometers can also be used as temperature sensors.
